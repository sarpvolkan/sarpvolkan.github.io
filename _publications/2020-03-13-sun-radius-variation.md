We previously detected sudden shifts in solar radious mesaurements between 1950–1970 (a period spanning the historic **Solar Cycle 19**, which reached an interesting peak exceeding 250 sunspot numbers in the yearly mean of S_n counts, a record unseen since **Solar Cycle 2** in the 1780s). While this behavior was initially attributed to an instrumental issue, the possible role of long-periodic variations in **ionospheric refractivity irregularity** may worth studying.
