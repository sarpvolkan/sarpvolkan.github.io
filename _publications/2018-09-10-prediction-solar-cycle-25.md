Using concepts from nonlinear dynamical systems and state-space reconstruction, We developed a data-driven forecasting framework based on time-delay embedding and simplex projection to predict the evolution of Solar Cycle 25. The model produced one of the earliest forecasts indicating that Solar Cycle 25 would be significantly stronger than the prevailing scientific consensus—a prediction that has since been supported by the observed progression of the solar cycle.

Furthermore, we resolved a sampling time-step dependency in simplex projection forecasts of the solar cycle. A systematic sensitivity analysis is underway to evaluate this across distinct solar cycle phases (ascending, maximum, descending, and minimum).
